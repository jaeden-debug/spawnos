SpawnOS — the free tools platform built by [Blackwater Aquatics Canada](https://blackwateraquatics.ca) — takes a different approach: a connected suite of science-grade aquarium calculators that use real bioload, chemistry, and volume math, draw on the same species database, and link into the guides that explain them. Browse them all at [/tools](/tools), and for the platform overview see [What Is SpawnOS](/lab-notes/what-is-spawnos).

## Why These Tools Are Different

Three things set the SpawnOS calculators apart from the typical one-box web tool:

- **Real math, not rules of thumb.** The stocking tool uses bioload, not the misleading "one inch per gallon" rule; the chemistry tools use actual conversions and targets.
- **Connected to the data.** The calculators read from the same [species database](/species) the rest of SpawnOS uses, so a compatibility or stocking answer reflects real species parameters.
- **Built by aquarists.** Blackwater Aquatics keeps and breeds fish, and the tools reflect how the math actually matters in a real tank — paired with guides that explain the "why."

## The Tools That Matter Most

A few are worth calling out because they fix the most common mistakes:

- **Stocking Density Calculator** ([/tools/stocking-density](/tools/stocking-density)) — the antidote to the "one inch per gallon" myth, which ignores body mass, waste, and adult size. Real bioload math prevents the overstocking that quietly degrades water quality and drives aggression.
- **Fish Compatibility Checker** ([/tools/fish-compatibility](/tools/fish-compatibility)) — scores a pairing across water parameters, temperament, size/predation, and habitat, and explains the result. Pair it with the [compatibility database](/compatibility) and the [fish compatibility guide](/compatibility/fish-compatibility-guide).
- **Nitrogen Cycle Tracker** ([/tools/nitrogen-cycle](/tools/nitrogen-cycle)) — cycling a tank before adding fish prevents "new tank syndrome," the most common cause of early fish deaths. See the [aquarium community guide](/lab-notes/aquarium-community-guide).
- **GH/KH Converter** ([/tools/gh-kh-converter](/tools/gh-kh-converter)) — matching species to your actual water hardness prevents the slow, chronic stress that causes mysterious losses.
