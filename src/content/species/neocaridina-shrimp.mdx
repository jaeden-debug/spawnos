# Neocaridina Shrimp: Colour Variants and Colony Management

*Neocaridina davidi* is a single species with an extraordinary range of selectively bred colour forms — all genetically the same animal, all with identical care requirements, but displaying a spectrum from deep cherry red through electric blue, vivid yellow, orange, chocolate, and complex rili patterns. Understanding the genetics, colour grading, and management of different variants is what distinguishes the serious Neocaridina breeder from the casual keeper.

This guide is the companion guide to the **[Cherry Shrimp care guide](/species/cherry-shrimp)** — all core care information (water parameters, moult cycle, feeding, breeding) is covered there in full depth. This guide focuses on the specific colour variants, their grading systems, and how to maintain colour line integrity across multiple variant tanks.

---

## The Neocaridina Colour Wheel

All *Neocaridina davidi* variants trace back to the same original wild population. Selective breeding has amplified, redirected, or eliminated different pigmentation elements to produce the diverse colour catalogue available today.

**The major colour pigments in Neocaridina:**
- **Red/Orange carotenoids** — Concentrated in Cherry, Orange (Pumpkin), Bloody Mary, and related lines
- **Blue iridophore components** — Present in Blue Velvet, Blue Dream, Blue Carbon Rili
- **Yellow xanthophores** — Yellow Neon, Yellow Fire, Golden Back lines
- **Melanophores (black/brown)** — Dominant in Chocolate, Black Rose, Carbon Rili patterns
- **Leucophores (white)** — White Pearl, Snowball-related lines (though true Snowball is *Neocaridina cf. zhangjiajiensis*, a related species)
- **Rili pattern gene** — Creates clear/transparent "rili" banding where pigment is absent; combines with other colours to produce Carbon Rili (clear + black), Orange Rili (clear + orange), Blue Rili (clear + blue)

## Colour Variant Catalogue

### Red Variants

**Cherry Shrimp (Neocaridina davidi "Red"):**
→ See full guide: **[Cherry Shrimp](/species/cherry-shrimp)**

Grades: Sakura → High Grade Cherry → Painted Fire Red (PFR) → SSS Fire Red

**Bloody Mary / Red Velvet:**
A distinct red line selected for deep velvet-red colouration with a different optical quality than Cherry/PFR lines — a rich, matte burgundy-red rather than glossy cherry-red. Bred from the same base but divergently selected. Grades overlap with Cherry grade terminology but are usually kept as separate lines.

---

### Blue Variants

**Blue Velvet / Blue Dream (Neocaridina davidi "Blue"):**
Deep blue colouration ranging from mid-blue (Blue Velvet) to intense steel blue (Blue Dream). Blue Dream is typically the highest-grade form of this line, with dense, opaque blue coverage.

Grading: Blue Velvet → Blue Dream → High Grade Blue Dream (near-uniform opaque blue)

**Blue Carbon Rili:**
Blue combined with the rili gene — deep blue head and tail with a clear/transparent mid-section. Creates a distinctive banded appearance.

**Blue Jelly:**
Near-translucent blue; less pigment density than Blue Dream but striking in groups.

---

### Yellow Variants

**Yellow Neon / Neon Yellow (Neocaridina davidi "Yellow"):**
Vivid lemon-yellow to golden-yellow. High-grade specimens display full-body colour intensity from rostrum to tail. Lower grades show translucency on the lower body.

**Yellow Fire / Golden Back:**
Golden-yellow with a distinct bright line running along the dorsal surface (the "golden back" trait). Attractive patterning; the golden back gene can be combined with other colour lines.

**Pumpkin Orange:**
Orange colouration intermediate between yellow and red lines. Some classify as a separate line; others as lower-grade red or higher-grade yellow selection. Warm orange in colour, usually quite translucent at lower grades.

---

### Orange Variants

**Orange Neon / Orange Rili:**
Vivid orange, particularly striking in high-grade individuals. Orange Rili combines orange with the rili transparent banding gene.

**Tangerine Tiger** (technically a separate species hybridisation in some lines — care required not to hybridise with pure Neocaridina lines).

---

### Brown and Black Variants

**Chocolate (Neocaridina davidi "Chocolate"):**
Rich chocolate-brown colouration. Less flashy than red or blue lines but increasingly popular for naturalistic biotope setups. Look visually appropriate with Indian Almond leaf and botanical tanks.

**Black Rose:**
Deep black colouration selected from melanophore-dominant individuals. High-grade Black Rose individuals display dense, matte black coverage.

**Carbon Rili:**
Black/dark grey + rili transparent banding. The "carbon" refers to the charcoal-dark pigmented sections against clear mid-section.

---

### White/Clear Variants

**White Pearl:**
Cream to white colouration with visible internal organs in most individuals. The "pearl" refers to a subtle iridescence rather than true white pigmentation.

## Genetics and Colour Line Integrity

**The most important rule of multi-variant Neocaridina keeping:** all colour variants will interbreed freely and offspring will revert toward a "wild type" brownish olive in mixed-colour colonies within 4–6 generations.

**Cross-contamination scenarios to avoid:**
- Keeping Cherry (red) and Blue Dream (blue) in the same tank
- Adding "assorted Neocaridina" mixed bags to a dedicated colour line tank
- Sharing equipment (nets, plants) between different colour tanks without cleaning

**Managing multiple lines:** If keeping more than one colour variant, maintain entirely separate tanks, equipment, and water change tools per line. Even transferring a plant from a Cherry tank to a Blue tank introduces risk if a shrimplet hitchhikes on the plant.

**Culling:** For high-grade line maintenance, remove low-grade individuals (poor colour coverage, translucent) from breeding populations. This maintains grade over generations. Culled shrimp can be fed to pea puffers, clownfish, or other carnivorous species.

## Blue Dream vs. Blue Velvet: Breeding Note

Blue Dream and Blue Velvet are the same genetic line at different grade levels — like Cherry (low grade) vs. Painted Fire Red (high grade) in red lines. If you purchase "Blue Velvet" and selectively breed for colour intensity over 4–6 generations with careful culling, you can produce "Blue Dream" grade individuals. The grade naming is marketing convention, not separate species or lineage.

## Feeding

All Neocaridina variants have identical nutritional requirements. Biofilm grazing as the primary nutrition source, supplemented with:

- Commercial shrimp pellets/wafers (2–3x per week)
- Blanched vegetables (zucchini, spinach, kale)
- Leaf litter (Catappa leaves, Guava leaves) — biofilm base and food
- **Live Baby Brine Shrimp** for breeding conditioning — **[from Blackwater Aquatics](https://blackwateraquatics.ca/collections/live-foods)**
- **Microworms** for shrimplet growth — **[from Blackwater Aquatics](https://blackwateraquatics.ca/collections/live-foods)**
