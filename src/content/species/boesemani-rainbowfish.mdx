# Boesemani Rainbowfish: The Complete Care, Tank & Breeding Guide

The Boesemani rainbowfish is one of the most spectacular community fish in the hobby — a striking, two-tone shoaler split between deep blue-grey at the front and fiery orange at the back, shimmering as it cruises through open water. *Melanotaenia boesemani* is hardy, peaceful, and active, but it asks for two things many keepers don't expect: hard, alkaline water (the opposite of blackwater tetras), and patience, because young fish are drab and develop their famous colour only over months. Give it space, hard water, and time, and it becomes a dazzling centerpiece shoaler.

This guide is the complete reference: the Boesemani's biology, the hard water it prefers, why colour develops slowly, how to set up its tank, what to feed it, which tank mates suit it, and how to breed it.

---

## Species Overview

The Boesemani rainbowfish (*Melanotaenia boesemani*) is a rainbowfish in the family Melanotaeniidae, native to West Papua, Indonesia. It reaches about 10 cm (4 inches), with a tall, laterally compressed body. Mature **males** are the showpieces, developing the species' signature sharp **two-tone pattern**: the front half a deep blue-grey to purple, the rear half a vivid orange-to-red, often with shimmering scales between. Females and juveniles are a more uniform silvery colour.

The Boesemani is **hardy**, **peaceful**, **active**, and **long-lived** (5–8 years), making it an excellent large-community shoaler. Two things define its care: it prefers **hard, alkaline water** (unlike most popular community fish, which lean soft and acidic), and its **colour develops slowly** — young fish bought from a store are often drab and unimpressive, transforming over months into the brilliant adults the species is famous for. Patience and proper conditions are rewarded with one of the most beautiful shoaling fish in the hobby.

## Natural History and Origin

*Melanotaenia boesemani* comes from the **Ayamaru Lakes** region of West Papua, living in hard, alkaline, mineral-rich lakes and tributaries with clear water and vegetation. This **hard-water habitat** is the key distinction in its care: it thrives in the harder, alkaline water that soft-water blackwater fish dislike, making it a great fish for keepers with hard tap water.

In the wild, Boesemani rainbowfish form **active shoals** in open water, feeding on insects, small invertebrates, and plant matter. Like many rainbowfish, they develop colour with age, maturity, and good conditions, and males display intensely to one another and to females, "firing up" their colours in a remarkable way during courtship and rivalry. The species is endangered in its limited natural range due to over-collection and habitat issues, but it is now widely and successfully tank-bred, producing hardy, adaptable fish — though buyers should be aware that young tank-bred fish need time to colour up.

## Water Parameters — Hard and Alkaline

| Parameter | Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Temperature | 75–82°F (24–28°C) | Warm tropical. |
| pH | 7.0–8.0 | **Neutral to alkaline** — prefers harder, alkaline water. |
| Hardness (GH) | 8–20 dGH | **Moderately hard to hard** — the key requirement. |
| Carbonate hardness (KH) | 5–15 dKH | Buffers the higher pH. |
| Ammonia / Nitrite | 0 ppm | Keep the tank cycled. |
| Nitrate | < 20 ppm | Keep reasonable with water changes. |

The Boesemani's preference for **hard, alkaline water** sets it apart from most popular community fish — it's ideal for keepers with hard tap water who struggle to keep soft-water tetras. It's adaptable but shows its best colour and health in moderately hard, neutral-to-alkaline conditions. ## Tank Setup Guide

### Tank size

Because they reach 10 cm and are very active swimmers, Boesemani rainbowfish need a **minimum of 30 gallons (115 litres)**, with 50+ gallons better, and **length is critical** — they need a long tank with open swimming room for their constant cruising. Always keep a shoal of 6 or more with both sexes, which also encourages the males to "fire up" their colour in display.

### Aquascape — open water and light

Provide **open swimming space** framed by background and side planting, driftwood, and rocks. A planted tank with a clear central swimming area suits them well; they're not destructive to plants. Unlike blackwater fish, Boesemani appreciate **brighter lighting**, which (with maturity and good food) helps develop and showcase their colour — morning sun or strong tank lighting often triggers the most intense "firing up" displays.

### Filtration, flow, lid

Boesemani rainbowfish are active and appreciate **moderate-to-strong flow** and good filtration with high oxygenation, reflecting their open-water lake origins. A **lid is important**, as active rainbowfish can jump. A long, well-lit, well-filtered tank with swimming room and a shoal brings out their best.

## Feeding Guide

Boesemani rainbowfish are omnivores with hearty appetites that take a wide variety of foods, and a varied diet is important for developing their colour.

### What to feed

- **Quality flake and pellets** — a convenient staple (they're large enough for standard sizes).
- **Live and frozen [daphnia](/species/daphnia)**, [baby brine shrimp](/species/baby-brine-shrimp), and bloodworm — relished, excellent for colour development and conditioning.
- **Vegetable/spirulina** content — they take plant matter and benefit from it; colour-enhancing foods help bring out the orange.

### How often

Feed **two to three meals daily** of a varied diet. Good, varied nutrition (with colour-enhancing and live/frozen foods) is one of the main factors — alongside maturity, hard water, and good light — that develops the males' brilliant two-tone colour. A healthy Boesemani is full-bodied, active, and increasingly colourful as it matures.

## Behavior and Temperament

Boesemani rainbowfish are **peaceful, active, and gregarious** — they cruise the open water in a loose shoal, constantly on the move, and the males perform spectacular **"firing up" displays**, intensifying their blue and orange and flashing a shimmering "courtship stripe" on the forehead to impress females and out-display rival males. A shoal of mature males trading displays is a genuine spectacle.

They are completely peaceful toward other species, though their size and activity mean they're best with similarly active, robust tank mates rather than tiny timid fish. Keeping a **group with multiple males** encourages the colour-enhancing display behaviour (and spreads any minor male rivalry harmlessly). Their activity, peacefulness, and developing brilliance make them an outstanding centerpiece for a larger community.

## Compatibility

Boesemani rainbowfish are excellent peaceful community fish for larger, active tanks.

**Good tank mates:** [congo tetra](/species/congo-tetra), [denison barb](/species/denison-barb), [siamese algae eater](/species/siamese-algae-eater), [corydoras](/species/corydoras), [bristlenose pleco](/species/bristlenose-pleco), larger peaceful tetras and rasboras, [clown loach](/species/clown-loach), and other active, robust community fish. They also suit hard-water tanks where soft-water fish would struggle.

**Cautions:**

- **Tiny nano fish** — may be outcompeted or intimidated by the larger, active rainbows.
- **Aggressive cichlids** — clash with the peaceful rainbows.
- **Fin-nippers** — avoid.
- **Slow long-finned fish** — may struggle to compete with their energy.

Use the [compatibility checker](/tools/fish-compatibility) and the [stocking calculator](/tools/stocking-density) — Boesemani rainbows pair beautifully with other active, hard-water-tolerant species in a roomy tank.

## Breeding Guide

Boesemani rainbowfish are **egg-scatterers** and can be bred with patience. **Sexing** is clear in mature fish: males are taller-bodied and brilliantly two-toned; females are smaller and silvery. Note that you need **mature, coloured-up fish** to breed, which takes time.

Condition a group on rich, varied foods, then provide a spawning mop or fine-leaved plants ([Java moss](/species/java-moss) works) in hard, alkaline water. Rainbowfish are **continuous spawners** that lay a few eggs each day over an extended period, the eggs attaching to the mop/plants by fine threads. The eggs are not eaten as readily as some species', but for best yields, move the mop (with eggs) to a separate rearing tank every few days, or harvest eggs. The eggs take **1–2 weeks to hatch** (longer than many fish), and the tiny fry need very small first foods — [infusoria](/species/infusoria) and [green water](/species/green-water), then microworms and [baby brine shrimp](/species/baby-brine-shrimp). The slow-hatching eggs and tiny fry make it a patient project, but a rewarding one, and the gradual colour development of the growing young is a joy to watch.

## Health and Disease

Boesemani rainbowfish are hardy, and disease is uncommon with good care.

**Ich** can follow temperature swings or stress; treat promptly. **Bacterial and fungal infections** follow poor water or injury. They're active fish that need good oxygenation and clean water; chronically poor conditions or too-small a tank cause stress and dull colour. Newly bought young fish are simply **drab until they mature** — not a health problem, just a matter of time and good conditions.

Prevention: a **stable, cycled, hard, alkaline tank** with good filtration, flow, and oxygenation; swimming room; a proper shoal; a varied colour-enhancing diet; bright-ish lighting; a secure lid; and quarantine of new arrivals. Given those, the Boesemani is a robust, long-lived, increasingly brilliant fish.

## Interesting Facts

- **Patience pays.** Young Boesemani are drab silver and develop their famous two-tone blue-and-orange only over months of maturity and good conditions — don't judge them in the store.
- **They "fire up."** Males intensify their colour and flash a shimmering forehead "courtship stripe" during display and rivalry, an electric spectacle.
- **Hard-water lovers.** Unusually among popular community fish, they prefer hard, alkaline water — ideal for hard-tap-water keepers.
- **Endangered but tank-bred.** Wild populations are threatened in their tiny native range, but the species is now widely and successfully aquacultured.
- **Continuous spawners.** Like most rainbowfish, they lay a few thread-anchored eggs daily over an extended period rather than in one batch.

## Bringing It Together

The Boesemani rainbowfish is a spectacular, hardy, peaceful centerpiece shoaler — once you provide the two things it asks for: **hard, alkaline water** and **patience** for its slow-developing two-tone brilliance. Give it a long 30-gallon-plus tank with open swimming space, hard alkaline water, moderate-to-strong flow and good oxygenation, bright-ish lighting, a varied colour-enhancing diet, a secure lid, and a shoal of 6 or more with multiple males — and over months it will transform into a dazzling blue-and-orange showpiece that "fires up" in display. It's a perfect choice for hard-water tanks and pairs beautifully with [congo tetras](/species/congo-tetra), [denison barbs](/species/denison-barb), and [corydoras](/species/corydoras).
