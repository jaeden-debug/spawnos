## What SpawnOS Is

SpawnOS is an aquarium operating system — a single place where the calculations, species data, and breeding intelligence a serious aquarist needs actually live together. Most hobby resources are scattered: a forum thread for parameters, a blog for compatibility, a spreadsheet for breeding logs, a half-remembered rule of thumb for stocking. SpawnOS replaces that scatter with one interconnected platform where your fish, pairs, spawns, water parameters, and predictions reference each other.

The public side is free and needs no account. That includes the [aquarium calculators](/tools), the [species database](/species), the [fish compatibility checker](/tools/fish-compatibility), the [Library](/library) of deep reference databases, and the [AI assistant](/blueprints). > **The short version:** SpawnOS turns the scattered knowledge of the hobby into a connected system. Blackwater Aquatics Canada makes the living side — the cultures, the live foods, the fish. SpawnOS makes the intelligence side — the math, the data, and the decisions.

## The Aquarium Tools

The [SpawnOS calculator suite](/tools) is a set of free, science-based tools that replace the rules of thumb most aquarists rely on. None of them require an account, and each one explains its reasoning.

The [stocking density calculator](/tools/stocking-density) answers "how many fish can my tank hold?" using real bioload math — tank volume, filtration, and species-specific load — instead of the misleading one-inch-per-gallon rule. The [nitrogen cycle tracker](/tools/nitrogen-cycle) takes your ammonia and nitrite readings and tells you where you are in the cycle and what to do next. The [water change calculator](/tools/water-change) predicts nitrate concentration after each change and tells you how many changes you need to hit a target.

On the chemistry side, the [pH buffer calculator](/tools/ph-buffer) doses sodium bicarbonate, crushed coral, or peat to a target pH; the [GH/KH converter](/tools/gh-kh-converter) translates between dGH, dKH, ppm, and mmol/L; the [salt dosage calculator](/tools/salt-dosage) handles disease treatment, brackish prep, and marine mixing; and the [medication calculator](/tools/medication-calculator) builds dosing schedules for kanamycin, metronidazole, copper, ich treatments, and praziquantel with species safety warnings.

For hardware and environment, the [heater size calculator](/tools/heater-size), [filter size calculator](/tools/filter-size), [lighting calculator](/tools/lighting-calculator), [tank volume calculator](/tools/tank-volume), and [temperature converter](/tools/temperature-converter) size the equipment to the tank. The [feeding calculator](/tools/feeding-calculator) prevents overfeeding — the leading cause of water quality problems — and the [water parameter reference](/tools/water-parameters) lets you compare ideal ranges across species at a glance.

## Fish Compatibility Intelligence

The question "can these fish live together?" is the most common one in the hobby, and most answers reduce it to a useless yes/no. SpawnOS treats compatibility as a set of conditions, not a verdict.

The [fish compatibility checker](/tools/fish-compatibility) scores any two species across the four factors that actually decide cohabitation: water parameter overlap, temperament, size disparity, and predation risk. It returns a 0–100 score, a relationship type (tankmates, conditional community, predator/prey, live food, do-not-mix), a parameter-overlap breakdown, risk flags, and a recommended setup — minimum tank size, flow, planting, and hiding. It explains *why* a pairing works or fails, which is the whole point.

If you want the full theory behind those scores, the [complete fish compatibility guide](/compatibility/fish-compatibility-guide) is the pillar reference — 4,000+ words on parameters, temperament, the predator-prey size rule, and tank design. And the [compatibility database](/compatibility) holds scored, written-up pairings for popular combinations like [betta and shrimp](/compatibility/betta-and-shrimp) and [betta and corydoras](/compatibility/betta-and-corydoras).

## Breeder Intelligence

This is where SpawnOS earns the "operating system" name. The breeder workspace is built for keepers who want to run a fishroom like a laboratory — and everything in it is connected.

A **fish** record holds the genetics, parameters, and notes for an individual. Fish connect to **pairs**; a pair connects to a **compatibility and genetics prediction** and to the **spawns** it produces. A spawn connects to **fry survival tracking**, **logs**, and **offspring**, which feed back into **lineage**. Build a pair and SpawnOS predicts the outcome before you commit eggs to a tank; log a spawn and it tracks survival curves you can compare across attempts; raise the fry and they enter the lineage tree as the next generation's breeding stock.

That interconnection is the difference between a breeding spreadsheet and a breeding system. A spreadsheet stores what happened. SpawnOS connects what happened to what to do next — which pairs to repeat, which lines to keep, where survival dropped and why. The genetics engine is deterministic and explainable: it shows the reasoning behind every prediction, with warnings and recommendations a breeder can act on.
